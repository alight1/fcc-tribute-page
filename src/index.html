Jóhann Jóhannsson

"People seem to need labels, but they can be needlessly reductive"

His music in one word: profound

"I'm obsessed with the texture of sound, and interested in minimal forms, with how to say things as simply as possible, how to distill things into their primal form."

A brief history of Jóhann's life and key works

Birth
19-Sep-1969
Born in Reykjavík, Iceland to Jóhann Gunnarsson, a maintenance engineer who worked for IBM, and Edda Thorkelsdóttir.
1980s
1980
Started learning the piano and trombone, which he gave up in his teenage years.
1988
Co-founded Daisy Hill Puppy Farm, which released two singles between 1988 and 1990
1990s
1999
  • Co-founded Kitchen Motors, to help break down artistic borders.
  • Co-founded the Icelandic electronic group Apparat Organ Quartet.
Jóhann found his voice during those musical experimentations.
2000s
2002
Englabörn
First solo album
Pitchfork described it as "exceptionally restrained, the piano moving like droplets off of slowly melting icicles, the violin breathing warmth from above. The hesitation of each breath and falling bead feels as though it were a Morton Feldman piece condensed to three minutes."
