Jóhann Jóhannsson

"People seem to need labels, but they can be needlessly reductive"

His music in one word: profound

A brief history of Jóhann's life and key works

Pitchfork Media wrote of his first album, Englabörn (released in 2002):

"[it's] exceptionally restrained, the piano moving like droplets off of slowly melting icicles, the violin breathing warmth from above. The hesitation of each breath and falling bead feels as though it were a Morton Feldman piece condensed to three minutes."
